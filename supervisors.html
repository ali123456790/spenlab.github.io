Meet Our Lead Academic Supervisor

Dr. Ahmed Sherif, Senior Member, IEEE

Associate Professor, School of Computing Sciences & Computer Engineering,
University of Southern Mississippi (USM)

Dr. Ahmed Sherif is the driving force behind Spen Lab’s research initiatives. As the founder and director of the Security and Privacy of Emerging Networks (SPEN) Research Lab at USM, he leads a diverse team of graduate and undergraduate students in pioneering research across several cutting-edge fields.

Academic and Professional Background

  • Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering – Tennessee Technological University (2017)
  • M.S. – Egypt Japan University of Science & Technology (2014)
  • B.S. – Al Azhar University (2007)

Dr. Sherif’s excellence in research has been recognized with the Aubrey Keith and Ella Ginn Lucas Endowment for Faculty Excellence Award (USM, 2022). He has also secured multiple internal and external grants to support his innovative projects.

Research and Publications

Dr. Sherif’s work has been featured in top-tier IEEE conferences and journals, including:

  • IEEE International Conference on Communications (IEEE ICC)
  • IEEE Vehicular Technology Conference (IEEE VTC)
  • IEEE Transactions on Dependable and Secure Computing (IEEE TDSC)
  • IEEE Transactions on Vehicular Technology (IEEE TVT)
  • IEEE IoT Journal

In addition to his research, he serves as a reviewer and editor for several prestigious journals and conferences, such as IEEE TVT, IEEE IoT Journal, IEEE TDSC, and the Journal of Peer-to-Peer Networking.

Areas of Expertise

  • Cybersecurity and Network Security
  • Machine Learning in Cybersecurity
  • Privacy-Preserving Schemes in Autonomous Vehicles (AVs)
  • Vehicular Ad Hoc Networks (VANETs)
  • Internet of Things (IoT) Applications
  • Smart Grid Advanced Metering Infrastructure (AMI) Networks

Professional Affiliations
